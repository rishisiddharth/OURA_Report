# OURA_Report
This is my health data from ring I wear, OURA. It tracks heart rate, metrics in sleep, and an activity score, made up of steps etc)>




**Readiness Score**
Readiness is your daily indicator of how well your body has recovered and how prepared you are for physical, mental, and emotional challenges.
A high readiness score means your body is well-rested and ready for demanding activity. A moderate score suggests you’re doing fine but should be mindful of your energy, while a low score indicates your body needs rest and recovery.

Purpose: Guides you to balance exertion and rest for optimal performance.

How it works: Combines overnight recovery data and long-term activity/sleep trends to detect strain, overtraining, or illness before you notice symptoms.

Interpretation:

85–100: Ready for challenges

70–84: Good, but monitor energy

Below 70: Prioritize rest

**Sleep Score**
Sleep measures both the quantity and quality of your rest.
It evaluates your sleep structure, including time spent in different sleep stages and how restful and efficient your sleep was.

Purpose: Helps you understand if your sleep supports full restoration and recovery.

How it works: Integrates total sleep time, deep and REM sleep, and circadian alignment to provide a holistic view of your nightly rest.

Interpretation:

85–100: Restorative, balanced sleep

70–84: Decent sleep, some room for improvement

Below 70: Disturbed or insufficient sleep

**Activity Score**
Activity tracks your daily movement, including both exercise and non-exercise activity.
It encourages consistent, balanced habits rather than overexertion or prolonged inactivity.

Purpose: Supports sustainable activity levels aligned with your body’s needs and recovery state.

How it works: Measures total movement, workout frequency, and rest balance to help you maintain healthy mobility.

Interpretation:

85–100: Active and well-recovered

70–84: Fairly active, but could improve balance

Below 70: Too inactive or overtrained
